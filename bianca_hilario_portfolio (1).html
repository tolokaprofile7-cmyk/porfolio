My Story

A Different Brain.
A Distinct Voice.

As an autistic senior graduating with a B.A. in Communication in May 2026, my academic journey has been one of persistence, growth, and self-discovery. College challenged me not only academically but personally—I had to find methods to succeed in an environment not always designed for the way my brain works.

"Social and verbal spontaneity never came easily. So I prepared thoroughly—index cards, structured thoughts, deliberate words. What started as a survival mechanism became my greatest competitive advantage."

That deliberateness now defines everything I produce. Whether it's a qualitative framing analysis, an interactive digital narrative, or an original advertising campaign—every piece is crafted with accuracy, intentionality, and structural attention to detail.

I have been placed on the Dean's List multiple semesters, received the New Haven Promise full four-year scholarship, been inducted into Lambda Pi Eta Communications Honor Society, and had my research selected for presentation at the Eastern Communication Association undergraduate conference in April 2026.

Outside academics, I mentor neurodivergent teens, fundraise for families in the Philippines, and perform as a flutist and keyboardist in the All City Honors Band. My goal is a career in advertising or marketing communications at organizations that genuinely value cognitive diversity.

Personal Statement

In My Own Words

I am a graduating senior who will hold a Bachelor of Arts Degree in Communication in May 2026, and I developed this entire portfolio with one belief: my brain is not broken to work around—it is a lens that focuses everything I produce.

Being an autistic professional, I have been taught to speak with a degree of accuracy, deliberateness, and structural attention to detail, which I have come to realize is one of my most significant competitive advantages. This commitment has earned me several consecutive Dean's List awards, a four-year New Haven Promise Scholarship, membership in the Communications National Honor Society, and an upcoming Academic Award at my university Senior Awards Night.

The work I wrote on the representation of autism in movies was selected to be presented at the undergraduate conference of the Eastern Communication Association—proof that a subjective approach to an issue can produce scholarship that is heard long after the classroom.

Outside of academics, I mentor neurodivergent teens and young children in my community, raise money for charity to support families in the Philippines, and have applied a lifelong passion in music as a flutist and keyboardist in the All City Honors Band—a creative sensibility that permeates all my work.

I want to be on an advertising or marketing communications team of a company that truly appreciates cognitive diversity, since I offer something uncommon: the capability to fluidly cross between rigorous data analysis, conceptual creative campaigns, and emotionally engaging storytelling—all informed by a viewpoint that is genuinely underrepresented in the field.

My portfolio does not merely represent a set of assignments. It is proof that neurodivergent professionals are not merely compatible with great communications teams—we create them.

Original Creative Work

Pass It On Campaign Poem

Artist's Note

Written as the centerpiece of my Pass It On values campaign, this original poem speaks in the first person of an autistic college senior graduating in spring 2026.

It is formatted for a two-minute video presentation, paired with personal photographs and text overlays. The structure is intentional: seven stanzas move from confusion and self-doubt through resilience, community, and a call to action.

Every line break is a breath. Every stanza transition is a tonal shift designed to earn the final tagline before it arrives.

A note on voice: I chose not to voice-record this poem. Alongside being autistic, I was born with shortened tongue muscles, which makes clear speech a challenge—though I have completed oral presentations before. Writing is where I communicate with the most precision and power.
Engage and Transform
Between these walls of learning, I carved my space,
Dog-eared pages, stubborn hope, an unsteady grace.
A world of color, noise, and spinning light—
Some mornings I just asked myself: is this worth the fight?
Lectures moved like rivers, tests rose like walls,
I tripped on the transitions, stumbled down the halls.
But falling taught me something textbooks never could:
that rising, even slowly, counts as something good.
The chaos had a rhythm once I learned its name,
not a flaw to fix—just a different kind of flame.
I stopped rehearsing silence. I let my voice ring out,
and found that it was sharper for surviving all the doubt.
I built my circle slowly—quality, not size—
people who could see me past the mask behind my eyes.
We traded laughs and late nights, traded struggle and repair,
proof that belonging isn't given; it's grown by those who dare.
And still I kept on turning every hard page to the next,
not for any grade point, but for something I had wexed—
a knowing in my chest that I belonged in every room,
that my wiring, wide and tangled, was also how I bloom.
So here I walk across this stage, diploma in my hand,
not despite the way my brain is built—because of how I'm planned.
Autism shaped the artist, the thinker, and the drive,
but I am every story that I chose to keep alive.
To every dreamer wired different, reading this today:
the path is rough, the journey long—walk it anyway.
What got you here will carry you; your difference is your light.
Perseverance…… Pass It On.
